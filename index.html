How I evaluate series, creative engines, and platform strategy.

I’m drawn to series with durable engines and distinctive voice. I am particularly interested in how character psychology sustains long-term engines. These breakdowns reflect how I think about development: sustainability, tone control, audience alignment, and franchise potential.

Format: Thesis • Engine • Voice • Comps • Audience • Takeaway

The Last of Us (HBO)

Thesis

A prestige character drama layered onto a survival genre engine.

Engine

Journey-based structure with episodic character encounters.

Voice

Emotionally restrained, grounded, intimacy within spectacle.

Comps

Prestige survival drama, IP done character-first.

Audience

Elevated IP adaptation that translated a gaming property into character-driven prestige television.

Takeaway

Adaptations work when character drives genre.

Creative Patterns I’m Tracking

  • Prestige with a procedural spine: elevated storytelling supported by repeatable engines.

  • Recognizable IP with fresh POV: adaptation as reinvention, not replication.

  • Specificity over universality: hyper-defined worlds that scale through authenticity.

  • Antihero narratives evolving: toward consequence and psychological cost.

  • Global-first concepts: designed for cultural travelability without losing voice.
